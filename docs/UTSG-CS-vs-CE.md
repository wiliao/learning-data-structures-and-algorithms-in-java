## Quick Comparison

| | Computer Science | Computer Engineering |
|---|---|---|
| **Faculty** | Faculty of Arts & Science | Faculty of Applied Science & Engineering |
| **Degree** | HBSc (Honours Bachelor of Science) | BASc (Bachelor of Applied Science) |
| **Program types** | Major, Minor, Specialist | Specialist (only) |
| **OUAC code** | Apply to Arts & Science first (no direct CS code) | **TCS** |
| **Admission type** | Conditional entry → post-first-year application to CS POSt | Direct entry from high school |
| **Supplemental application (high school)** | ✅ Required (4 questions, choose 3, 120 words each) | ✅ Required |
| **Work-integrated learning** | ASIP (Arts & Science Internship Program) | PEY Co-op |
| **Typical degree length with co-op** | 5 years | 5 years |
| **Engineering designation (P.Eng.)** | ❌ No | ✅ Yes |
| **AI/research ecosystem access** | Vector Institute, CS labs | Vector Institute, ECE labs, SciNet, COHESA |

> ⚠️ **Correction from common misconceptions:** UTSG CS *does* require a supplemental application at the high school stage, similar to CE. Both programs are competitive from the outset.

## Admission Requirements (Ontario High School)

### Computer Science

| Subject | Required Course |
|---|---|
| English | ENG4U / EAE4U |
| Advanced Functions | MHF4U |
| Calculus & Vectors | MCV4U |
| Math of Data Management | **Recommended** (MDM4U) |
| Supplemental Application | **Required** (4 questions, choose 3 to answer, 120 words each) |

**Typical admitted average:** Mid-to-high 90s for UTSG. CS at St. George is among the most competitive programs in Canada — approximately 1 in 16 applicants are admitted.

**Important — Two-stage admission process:**

You are **not admitted directly to CS**. You are admitted to the Faculty of Arts & Science first, then apply to a Computer Science POSt (Program of Study) at the end of first year.

**Guaranteed CS POSt admission requirements (first-year gate):**
- Complete at least 4.0 full course equivalents (FCEs)
- Complete **CSC110Y1** with a mark of **≥ 70%**
- Complete **CSC111H1** with a mark of **≥ 77%** (Specialist or Major) or **≥ 70%** (Minor)
- Complete **MAT137Y1** or **MAT157Y1** (Specialist or Major only)
- Submit program request on ACORN during the designated period

> ⚠️ These thresholds guarantee admission to CS POSt *only if met*. Failing to meet them means competing for limited spots in other categories, or being locked out of the CS Specialist/Major entirely. This is a genuine risk and should factor into your decision.

### Computer Engineering

| Subject | Required Course |
|---|---|
| English | ENG4U / EAE4U |
| Advanced Functions | MHF4U |
| Calculus & Vectors | MCV4U |
| Chemistry | **SCH4U** (required) |
| Physics | **SPH4U** (required) |

**Admission type:** Direct entry via OUAC code **TCS**. You are in the CE program from day one — no internal application gate after first year.

**Typical admitted average:** Mid-to-high 90s. Requires both Chemistry and Physics at Grade 12 level, which CS does not.

## Curriculum & Focus

### Computer Science (Arts & Science)

- **Software and theory focused** — algorithms, programming languages, AI/ML, computational linguistics, computer vision, HCI
- **Very flexible** — Major, Minor, or Specialist options. You can double-major or combine CS with virtually any Arts & Science field (biology, linguistics, economics, physics, etc.)
- **World-renowned AI heritage:** Geoffrey Hinton (Nobel Prize, "Godfather of AI"), Raquel Urtasun, and Sanja Fidler (VP AI Research at NVIDIA) are all UofT CS faculty — co-founders of the Vector Institute. UofT CS ranked **3rd globally for AI** (ARWU 2025 Subject Rankings) and **#1 in Canada** (QS, Times Higher Education, Shanghai Ranking).

**Program structure by type:**

| Type | Scope |
|---|---|
| Minor | Introduction to theoretical and applied CS; up to three 300+ level courses |
| Major | Builds on Minor; prepares for upper-year study; integrates with another discipline |
| Specialist | Broad and deep foundation; exposes students to wide range of upper-year CS topics |

**Optional Focus areas** (for Major or Specialist students):
- Artificial Intelligence
- Computational Linguistics & Natural Language Processing
- Computer Systems
- Computer Vision
- Game Design
- Human-Computer Interaction
- Scientific Computing
- Theory of Computation
- Web & Internet Technologies
- Data Science (combined CS + Statistics specialist)

**First-year courses (representative):**
- CSC110Y1: Foundations of Computer Science I
- CSC111H1: Foundations of Computer Science II
- MAT137Y1 or MAT157Y1: Calculus (proof-based)

### Computer Engineering (Engineering)

- **Hardware + software focused** — digital systems, computer architecture, embedded systems, VLSI design, networking, electronics, alongside programming and algorithms
- **Rigid specialist track** — fixed course sequence with less elective flexibility compared to CS
- **Leads to P.Eng. designation** — graduates are eligible for professional engineering licensure in Canada
- **Direct entry** — you're in the program from day one with a defined cohort
- **Research ecosystem:** CE students have access to the **Vector Institute**, **SciNet** (UofT's supercomputing consortium), and **COHESA** (hardware-optimized AI research network with AMD, Intel, Google, IBM, Microsoft as partners), as well as the Fujitsu Co-Creation Research Laboratory
- **ECE department strength:** 79 professors, 42 IEEE Fellows, 10 Fellows of the Royal Society of Canada, 3 University Professors (UofT's highest honour)

## Experiential Learning (Co-op / Internship)

This is one of the most practically important differences between the two programs.

| | CS — ASIP | CE — PEY Co-op |
|---|---|---|
| **Full name** | Arts & Science Internship Program | Professional Experience Year Co-op |
| **Duration** | 12–20 months | 12–16 months |
| **Entry** | Competitive (limited enrolment) | Open to eligible CE students |
| **When** | Typically between Year 2–3 or Year 3–4 | Typically after Year 2 or Year 3 |
| **Degree length** | Normally extends to **5 years** | Normally extends to **5 years** |
| **Additional fees** | ✅ Yes — supplemental fees paid over 6 installments | Minimal (part-time incidental fees while working) |
| **Job board access** | Dedicated ASIP co-op board | Engineering Career Centre (ECC) PEY portal |
| **Sample employers (CS ASIP)** | BMO, RBC, TD, CIBC, Scotiabank, CPP, HOOPP, Ontario Teachers' | Amazon, IBM, Intel, NVIDIA (US), Rapyuta Robotics (Switzerland) |
| **Employment guaranteed?** | ❌ No — students must secure positions | ❌ No — students must apply and be selected |

**Key distinction:** ASIP is competitive-entry within CS — not all CS students qualify or gain a spot. PEY is available to all CE students in good academic standing. Both extend your degree to approximately 5 years. ASIP has additional fees; PEY does not beyond part-time incidental fees.

**Important for AI/ML goals:** CE's PEY portal has historically included placements at hardware and AI-infrastructure companies (NVIDIA, Intel, IBM). CS ASIP employers skew more toward financial institutions and software companies, though top tech firms also recruit through both portals.

## AI & Research Ecosystem

Both programs sit within one of the world's most important AI research environments. This is a UofT-wide advantage regardless of your program.

| Resource | CS Access | CE Access |
|---|---|---|
| **Vector Institute** | ✅ Directly affiliated (CS faculty co-founded it) | ✅ Access via Engineering partnerships |
| **SciNet Supercomputing** | ✅ | ✅ |
| **COHESA** (hardware-optimized AI network with AMD, Intel, Google) | Partial | ✅ Direct |
| **Fujitsu Co-Creation Research Lab** | — | ✅ |
| **Schwartz Reisman Institute** | ✅ | ✅ |
| **UTIAS Robotics** | — | ✅ (closer collaboration) |

**Notable AI faculty connections:**
- **Geoffrey Hinton** — Nobel Prize laureate, "Godfather of AI," UofT CS Professor Emeritus, Vector Institute co-founder
- **Raquel Urtasun** — UofT CS, Vector Institute co-founder, autonomous vehicles research
- **Sanja Fidler** — UofT CS, VP AI Research at NVIDIA, Vector Institute co-founder
- **Nicolas Papernot** — ECE/CS joint, 2025 Steacie Prize winner, AI security research

> For an undergraduate interested in AI/ML, the practical implication is: **CS gives you slightly closer proximity to pure ML theory research labs; CE gives you closer proximity to hardware-accelerated AI and systems research labs.** Both are world-class environments.

## Community & Student Life

### Computer Science

- Part of the **Faculty of Arts & Science** — access to all Arts & Science student resources
- Assigned to one of **seven college communities** (University College, Trinity, Victoria, St. Michael's, New, Innis, Woodsworth) — each with its own residence, academic advising, and clubs
- More diverse peer group — CS students mix with students from many other disciplines
- Flexible first year allows exploration before committing to CS

### Computer Engineering

- Part of the **Faculty of Applied Science & Engineering** — shared first-year engineering curriculum with all Core 8 engineering programs
- **Strong engineering culture** — Engineering Society (EngSoc), design teams (U of T Robotics, Blue Sky Solar Racing, etc.), engineering competitions
- **Tighter cohort** — you move through the program largely with the same group from Year 2 onward
- **Design teams throughout** — hands-on project-based learning from first year, culminating in a year-long capstone project

## Key Takeaways

| Factor | Advantage |
|---|---|
| Maximum flexibility (double majors, electives) | **CS** |
| Direct entry, no first-year gate risk | **CE** |
| P.Eng. professional designation | **CE** |
| Pure ML/theory research proximity | **CS** (slight) |
| Hardware-accelerated AI / systems research | **CE** |
| Broader master's program access (SCS, Stanford CS) | **CS** (slight) |
| EECS/ECE/Robotics master's pathways | **CE** (slight) |
| Co-op availability (all students) | **CE** (PEY; ASIP is competitive-entry) |
| AI ecosystem access (Vector Institute etc.) | **Both equally** |

**Choose CS if:**
- You want maximum curriculum flexibility and the ability to pair CS with another discipline
- Your goal is pure software engineering, ML theory, or academic AI research
- You don't have Chemistry/Physics at Grade 12 level
- You're comfortable with the first-year gate (and confident you'll meet CSC110/111 thresholds)

**Choose CE if:**
- You want direct entry with certainty from day one
- You're drawn to the intersection of hardware and software, or AI systems/infrastructure
- You want the P.Eng. designation
- You value a tight engineering cohort and hands-on design culture
- You have strong Physics and Chemistry

**The most important factor for either path:**
Attach yourself to a research lab by Year 2. UofT's AI and ECE research environments are world-class regardless of which program you're in. A strong research profile and GPA will determine your graduate school outcomes far more than the CE vs CS label.
